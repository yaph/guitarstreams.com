Buck Owens

Alvis Edgar Owens Jr., professionally known as Buck Owens, was an American musician, singer, songwriter and band leader. He was front man for Buck Owens and the Buckaroos, which had 21 No. 1 hits on the Billboard country music charts. It pioneered what came to be called the Bakersfield sound, named after Bakersfield, California, the city Owens called home and from which he drew inspiration for what he preferred to call 'American music'.

Genres: country music
Instruments: guitar, voice
Occupations: singer, singer-songwriter, guitarist, composer

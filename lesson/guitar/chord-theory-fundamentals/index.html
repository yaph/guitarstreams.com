Chord Theory Fundamentals

In this music theory lesson Steve Stine breaks down fundamental chord theory using the C major scale. Chords start out as triads, i. e. they consist of three notes in their basic form: the root, the third and the fifth. Taking all the notes in the C major scale as roots and building the corresponding chords from these notes we end up with seven different chords: three major, three minor and one diminished chord.

The whole process Steve walks us through can be applied to any major scale so it really pays off understanding this. If you shy away from music theory, take 20 minutes and watch Steve's lesson. The basics are simple and certainly nothing to be scared of. You'll see it.

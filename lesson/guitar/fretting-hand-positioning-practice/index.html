Fretting Hand Positioning Practice

GuitarLessons365 links: Website • Youtube • Facebook • Patreon • Twitter

In this lesson Carl Brown from GuitarLessons365 teaches a simple exercise that helps with positioning the fretting hand for playing well-sounding scales and bar chords. In these situations you want your palm to be parallel to the bottom of the neck and your thumb on the back.

You can practice proper positioning by baring the strings with your index finger and playing individual notes with the others. Watch Carl demonstrate this and provide you with several ways on how you can vary this exercise and practice hand positioning and scales at the same time.
